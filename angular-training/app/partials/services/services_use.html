• Inject it as a parameter in the dependent object (controller, service, directive)...
  • ... And Angular dependency injection system will take care of the rest.
  • It will handle service instantiation and dependencies resolution for you.
  • Warning: Use .$inject to identify services (elsewhere, problems will appear when minifying your js files).
  • You can also use the nodejs plugin ngmin, which will add dependency injection annotations for you.
